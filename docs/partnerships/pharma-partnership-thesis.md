## 2. The Pharma AI Governance Crisis

### AI Adoption is Accelerating

- **Drug discovery:** AI models identify drug candidates, predict molecular interactions, optimise trial designs
- **Clinical trials:** AI automates patient selection, monitors safety signals, predicts outcomes
- **Manufacturing:** AI drives predictive maintenance, quality control, batch optimisation
- **Commercial:** AI generates medical content, personalises HCP engagement, manages supply chain forecasting
- **Pharmacovigilance:** AI monitors adverse events across global markets in real-time

### But Governance Has Not Kept Pace

| Domain | AI Governance Challenge |
|--------|----------------------|
| Drug discovery | Which human approved this AI-generated molecular target? Was the training data validated? |
| Clinical trials | Can we prove the AI safety monitor was governed? Who authorised the dosing recommendation? |
| Manufacturing (GxP) | Did a qualified person approve this AI-driven batch release decision? Is the audit trail tamper-proof? |
| Commercial | Was this AI-generated medical content reviewed by a qualified medical advisor before distribution? |
| Pharmacovigilance | How do we prove the AI adverse event detection system was validated and monitored? |

### Regulatory Pressure is Intensifying

- **EU AI Act (2025):** AI systems in healthcare classified as "high-risk" — mandatory risk assessment, documentation, human oversight, audit trails
- **FDA AI/ML Guidelines:** Growing expectations for algorithm change protocols, real-world performance monitoring, and human oversight documentation
- **MHRA AI Framework:** UK medicines regulator developing AI-specific guidance for pharmaceutical AI
- **GxP Compliance (21 CFR Part 11):** Electronic records and signatures must be trustworthy, reliable, and equivalent to paper — AI decisions need the same standard
- **ICH Q9(R1):** Quality risk management now explicitly references AI-assisted decisions

### The Cost of Getting It Wrong

- **Regulatory delays:** FDA or EMA rejecting submissions that lack AI governance documentation
- **Product recalls:** AI-driven manufacturing decisions without adequate human oversight
- **Litigation risk:** Adverse events linked to AI decisions with no clear accountability chain
- **Reputational damage:** Public trust erosion if AI governance failures become public
- **Board liability:** Directors personally liable for AI failures they cannot evidence oversight of

## 8. Pharma AI Governance Use Cases (Detailed)

### Use Case 1: AI-Assisted Drug Discovery Governance

**Scenario:** J&J R&D uses an AI model to identify potential drug candidates from molecular databases.

**Without Verisum:**
- No structured record of which AI model was used, which training data, or who validated the output
- Regulatory submission includes AI-generated results but cannot evidence governance
- If the AI produces a false positive that enters clinical trials, no accountability chain exists

**With Verisum:**
- AI system registered in Verisum's AI Registry with model version, training data source, risk classification
- TrustSys assessment scores the AI system's governance maturity
- Human researcher signs off on AI recommendations via Verisum Verify — cryptographically signed approval
- Provenance certificate generated: "Drug candidate X identified by Model Y (v2.3), trained on Dataset Z, validated by Dr. A on [date]"
- Regulatory submission includes Verisum governance attestation as supporting evidence

### Use Case 2: GxP Manufacturing Quality Control

**Scenario:** AI system monitors manufacturing quality parameters and recommends batch release/reject decisions.

**Without Verisum:**
- AI recommendation logged in MES but no structured governance trail
- Qualified Person (QP) approval is a manual sign-off disconnected from AI decision
- If batch is recalled, no tamper-proof evidence of who approved what and when

**With Verisum:**
- AI QC system registered and assessed in Verisum (TrustSys)
- Real-time drift detection monitors AI system performance against baseline
- When AI recommends batch release, Verisum triggers QP approval workflow
- QP reviews AI recommendation and signs off via Verisum Verify — cryptographic signature
- Incident Lock activated if any quality event occurs — forensic freeze of all related governance data
- Full provenance: AI recommendation → QP review → signed approval → batch released → on-chain anchor

### Use Case 3: AI-Generated Medical Content Review

**Scenario:** AI drafts medical information leaflets, HCP communications, or social media content.

**Without Verisum:**
- AI-generated content reviewed informally via email chains
- No structured record of which AI generated what, who reviewed, what changes were made
- Regulatory risk if content reaches market without documented medical review

**With Verisum:**
- AI content generation system registered in Verisum
- Content routed through Verisum approval workflow: AI generates → Medical Advisor reviews → Legal reviews → Approval signed
- Each approval step cryptographically signed via Verify tier
- Provenance certificate: "Content piece X generated by AI Model Y, reviewed by Dr. B on [date], approved by Legal C on [date]"
- Full audit trail available for regulatory inspection
