## Narrative shift (before → after)

**Before:** "One Platform, Three Layers of Trust — Core = Govern, Assure = Monitor, Verify = Prove." Every tier locked to one module. Clean but underserves lower tiers and hides Verify's real moat inside "you get the whole Prove tab".

**After:** "Every tier gets Govern + Monitor + Prove. Depth changes, breadth doesn't. Chain-anchoring is the moat." Every user experiences all three pillars from day one; upgrades unlock deeper capability in areas they already understand.

### Section — "Three pillars, at every tier"

The old way of selling AI governance software gave you one capability per subscription tier — buy the "monitoring" tier, then discover you can't issue proofs. Verisum works the way governance actually works: **you need all three pillars from the start, and you grow each one at its own pace.**

#### Govern — Understand & document
Every tier includes the tools to understand your governance posture, document policies, and register the AI systems and vendors your organisation uses.
- **Explorer:** free self-assessment, 4 curated regulatory feeds, upload existing policies
- **Core:** unlimited surveys, AI policy generator, vendor register, incident logging basics
- **Assure:** editable AI policies, Models registry, advanced compliance frameworks
- **Verify:** unlimited everything, custom frameworks

#### Monitor — Watch for drift
Every paid tier includes at least basic monitoring of your own AI systems. Deeper Monitor unlocks with Assure and above.
- **Core:** Basic drift alerts on 2 systems, 3 incidents/month, staff declarations
- **Assure:** Full drift on 6 systems, escalation workflows, unlimited incidents, runtime signals
- **Verify:** Unlimited, custom escalation routing, priority alerting

#### Prove — Cryptographically demonstrate
Every tier can *verify* proofs sent to them. Paid tiers can record and issue their own. Chain-anchoring — the piece that makes proofs immutable and provable to auditors and regulators — is exclusive to Verify.
- **Explorer:** verify signed attestations sent to you (no signup required)
- **Core:** non-chain Decision Ledger, non-chain Incident Lock
- **Assure:** issue basic attestations, approvals workflow, Trust Exchange (limited)
- **Verify:** **on-chain anchored attestations, incidents, and provenance** — the category-of-one moat

### Section — "Why chain-anchoring matters"

When an auditor, regulator, or partner asks "was this AI decision approved by the right person, at the right time, under the right policy?" — chain-anchored proof is the difference between "here's our database" and "here's cryptographic evidence that cannot have been altered." Verisum Verify is the only AI governance platform where that evidence is anchored on-chain.

### FAQ

**Why does every tier include some Monitor and Prove capability?**
Because governance is not one pillar at a time. You need to understand your posture (Govern), watch for drift (Monitor), and provide evidence (Prove) from the moment you start. Verisum lets you experience the whole platform on any tier, and grow depth where you need it.

**What makes Verify different from Assure?**
Chain-anchoring. Assure gives you attestations, but they're stored in Verisum's database. Verify anchors them on-chain — creating cryptographic proof that no one, including Verisum, can alter. If you're subject to regulator audit, court proceedings, or high-stakes partner diligence, that difference matters.

**Can I try the platform before paying?**
Yes — Explorer is free forever. Take a self-assessment, verify any signed attestation someone sends you, and browse the compliance framework library.

**What's the annual discount?**
20% off any paid tier when billed annually.

**Do you offer a free trial of Core or Assure?**
Not currently — Explorer is our free tier, and it lets you experience the platform without a time limit.
